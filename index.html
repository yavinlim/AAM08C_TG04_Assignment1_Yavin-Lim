Welcome to Coney Island

A quiet and charming park by NParks Singapore

A unique park

Housing many species of birds and plants, Coney Island is one of Singapore's undeveloped parks. It does not contain many man-made structures and gives off a rustic and natural feeling that is definitely a unique experience.

Learn more

Guided walks

There will be two-hour guided walks conducted on selected Saturday mornings in the months of June, November and December by NParks volunteers for you to learn more about Coney Island.

Visiting Guidelines

1. The park is opened from 7am to 7pm.

2. Terrain is rugged at parts and may be unsuitable for young children and people with mobility issues.

3. Wear long pants and covered shoes for protection from insects such as sandflies.

4. Keep to the designated trails and refrain from entering the vegetated/ forested areas.

5. Appreciate any animals from afar.

6. Do not eat or carry plastic bags in the presence of macaques. Macaques may have been conditioned to associate plastic bags with food, and feeding them will make them bold.

7. Do avoid visiting the park during a storm, or seek shelter immediately if it rains while you are within the park.

8. Remember to take only what you have brought in, and leave nothing behind but footprints.

9. Please note that entry to the Haw Par Beach Villa is strictly not allowed.

Things to do

Bird Watching

Bird enthusiasts rejoice! Coney Island is a favoured stopover by migratory birds of different species. Be sure to catch them in action!

Nature Photography

Explore the fine details of the vast landscape in Coney Island by capturing it with the lenses of your cameras.

Cycling

Partake in an fun adventure around Coney Island with a breeze by cycling around the island. Bicycles are also available for rent.

Nature Walks

Reconnect with nature as you walk around the park and calm yourself from the hustle and bustle of the city. Be mindful as you explore and take in an unforgettable experience in Coney Island.
